Document your open source contributions at OpenHatch

This page explains how you can use OpenHatch to automatically document your contributions.

Automagically import your contributions from other networks

Your OpenHatch profile is where you can write about your involvement in free/open source projects. People contribute in lots of ways: code, documentation, translations, bug reports, packaging, and more.

The profile editor contains an importer to pull in what we can find via other websites. When you search for yourself, we examine project hosting sites and source code repositories. {% comment %}(You can see the current list of supported profile import sites .) {% endcomment %} The profile we put together combines the trails you've left behind on other websites.

Add what we missed

The OpenHatch importer can't find the patches you submitted to a mailing list, the work you did organizing a documentation sprint, and other kinds of non-committer contributions. In the profile editor, you can add projects and URLs that the importer didn't discovered automatically.

Your OpenHatch profile serves as a reminder to yourself of all the cool things you've done. It's also smart to maintain a portfolio of your free software work so you can put it on your resume.

Grab your widget

When your profile is ready, you can embed your OpenHatch profile on your own web things. You'll find a widget waiting in your personal settings.

There is no canonical project description

When you add a project to your profile, you appear on that project's page on OpenHatch. On that page, we take a random contributor's description of the project to give a voice to all the people involved.
